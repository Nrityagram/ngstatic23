Samhāra at
The MET

We are delighted to have shared with you our cherished experience of revisiting Samhāra at the Temple of Dendur. We have had the privilege of performing twice at the iconic Temple - an experience that we revisit innumerable times to bring us joy, most specially now during this pandemic.

In these times when millions of people across the world struggle to cope with anxiety of an uncertain future and the well-being of loved ones, there is one thing we all take refuge in. Art. Whether it is music, dance, poetry or painting, Art is what we all turn to, to fortify our spirit.

Yet for artists and art organisations like Nrityagram, who depend on community activity and live performances such as the one you just experienced, the Covid 19 crisis has caused our livelihood to come to a standstill. Our performances over the next six months are cancelled, workshops are suspended and, teaching and practice are compromised.

Training programmes are our smallest revenue stream and though our school is closed, we have endeavoured to keep classes going in whatever capacity possible. We are teaching online and our students are able to stay fit, train and rehearse.

While sweeping cancellations of regular activities have resulted in significant financial losses, we remain committed to supporting our teachers, performers and staff during this difficult time. We have paid salaries in full to everyone till now and are determined to continue to provide for all 30 people (wherever they may be), until we are through this crisis.

Though we aren’t sure when everyone will return to campus, we hope to perform and earn again by early 2021.

We need your help

As we create new ways to sustain ourselves, we seek your support to provide our artists a much needed security net to cope with such unforeseen shocks.

Please donate whatever you can - an amount as little as ₹1000 ($20) from each of you, will help us navigate this crisis. Your donation will be used to continue to pay salaries to teachers, dancers, production team members, and staff who make our year-round programmes possible, as well as cover the most basic expenses of our residential system.

We know this is a time of great hardship for many and we are grateful for whatever you can do to keep us on our feet.
